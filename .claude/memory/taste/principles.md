# Discovered Taste Principles

Each principle below was discovered through the Generate → Destroy loop.
They represent this project's emergent taste DNA.

---

- **Date**: 2026-04-06
- **Element**: Hero title glitch text effect
- **Principle**: "The title is a bureaucratic declaration, not a technological artifact — it must arrive with institutional authority, not mechanical process."
- **Evidence**: RGB-split glitch signals "cool web dev" not "government document." Typewriter/microfiche delays comprehension. Rubber stamp at title scale has no real-world analogue. First-gen photocopy misregistration is THE defining artifact of government doc reproduction.

---

- **Date**: 2026-04-06
- **Element**: Floating symbols component
- **Principle**: "Every decorative element must be physically plausible within the document fiction — if it cannot exist on a real desk, it does not belong on this page."
- **Evidence**: Floating triangles have no physical logic in a paper document world. Coffee stains don't drift. Torn paper doesn't levitate. Only static artifacts with physical origin stories belong (staples, paperclips, routing stamps).

---

- **Date**: 2026-04-06
- **Element**: Scroll reveal system
- **Principle**: "Content does not arrive — it is already present and becomes visible; animation serves reading rhythm, not theatrical entrance."
- **Evidence**: fadeInUp is the SaaS/portfolio default. Documents don't "come from below." Declassification-for-everything dilutes the redaction mechanic. Stamp-slam everywhere makes stamps meaningless. Opacity transition = gaining clearance to read.

---

- **Date**: 2026-04-06
- **Element**: Scroll progress bar
- **Principle**: "Navigation cues must feel like properties of the document itself, not of the reading interface — section labels, page numbers, and filing marks belong; progress bars and counters do not."
- **Evidence**: Gradient progress bar is a blog/Medium pattern. Classification escalation lies about content structure. Page counters and section labels are document-native. Real-time processing fiction contradicts a completed bulletin.

---

- **Date**: 2026-04-06
- **Element**: Threat meter
- **Principle**: "The fictional institution must be competent and authoritative — comedy comes from WHAT they take seriously, not from their failure to function."
- **Evidence**: Pulsing gradient implies fluctuation but threat is permanent. DEFCON borrows real military systems. Analog gauges foreground technology over institution. Static filled assessment grid = competent analyst who concluded MAXIMUM.

---

- **Date**: 2026-04-06
- **Element**: Repeated "CONFIRMED" on doctrine cards
- **Principle**: "Repeated institutional marks must vary to imply multiple handlers — uniformity exposes the single-author fiction."
- **Evidence**: Same stamp on 8 cards reads as template, not dossier. Real documents pass through multiple analysts with different stamps, dates, initials. Variation creates narrative depth (multiple people reviewed this).

---

## META-PRINCIPLE (synthesized from all 6):
**"Every pixel on this page must be an artifact of the fictional institution that produced it — not an artifact of the web developer who built it."**
